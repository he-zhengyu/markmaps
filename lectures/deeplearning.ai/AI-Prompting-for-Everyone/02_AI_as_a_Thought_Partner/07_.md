# AI Critique & Editing

## 1. Editing Techniques
* **Piece-by-Piece Editing**
    * Edit one sentence or paragraph at a time instead of the entire article.
    * Makes the editing workflow much more manageable.
* **Iterative Brainstorming**
    * Ask the AI for different stylistic variations (e.g., punchy, visionary, conversational).
    * Iterate until you nail down the text, then move to the next section.

## 2. Holistic Feedback & Overcoming Sycophancy
* **The Sycophancy Problem**
    * Without strict instructions, AI tends to blindly praise your work rather than objectively critique it.
* **The Solution: Use a Rubric**
    * Provide explicit grading criteria (e.g., Characters: 25 pts, Plot: 25 pts).
    * Define criteria unambiguously (e.g., "Every main character has a goal" = True/False).
    * Forces the AI to evaluate using an objective, well-specified standard.
    * *Tip:* You can ask the AI to help you brainstorm and create the rubric first.

## 3. Structuring the Critique Prompt
* **Good Prompts**
    * Provide the text + the detailed rubric.
    * Command the AI to be objective.
    * Instruct it to assign a score per category *first*, and then sum the scores at the end.
    * Ask for focused suggestions on how to improve the score.
* **Poor Prompts**
    * Asking to "score out of 100" with ambiguous categories causes the AI to leap to a score first and justify it later, usually resulting in inflated, biased scores.

## 4. Advanced Techniques
* **Cross-Model Review**
    * Have one AI model (e.g., Gemini) critique the output generated by a different model (e.g., ChatGPT).
    * Integrates knowledge from different systems and provides a reassuring, objective check.
* **Understanding "Jagged Intelligence"**
    * AI capabilities are uneven: they are far better than humans at some tasks, but much worse at others.
    * Different AI models are "jagged" in different ways.
* **Model Switching**
    * The AI marketplace is highly competitive and rapidly changing.
    * Routinely feed the same prompt to multiple models to build your intuition on which model is currently best for specific tasks.
