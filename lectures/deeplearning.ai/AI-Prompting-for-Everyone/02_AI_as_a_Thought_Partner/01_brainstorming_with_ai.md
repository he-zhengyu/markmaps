# Brainstorming with AI

## AI as a Thought Partner
- Useful when no **human expert** is available
- AI knows *a lot about a lot of things*
- 💡 Great for thinking through complex problems & decisions
- 📊 Usage stats from OpenAI on ChatGPT
  - ~50% of chats: **writing & practical guidance**
  - **3.9%** of chats: *creative ideation*

## Two Modes of Brainstorming

### Mode 1: Generate Many Options
- Ask AI for a **long list** of ideas
- Classic creativity test
  - 🔑 "Name 200 uses for a brick"
  - Hard for humans, easy for AI
  - Examples: paperweight, planter, building material
- Your role = **evaluate & pick** the good ones
- 💡 Principle: *more ideas → better odds of a great one*

### Mode 2: Iterate with Context
- Give AI **rich context** upfront
- Have a **longer back-and-forth** conversation
- Goal: push AI beyond generic answers

## Why Basic Prompts Give Generic Answers

### The Common-Sense Trap
- Example prompt: *"Help me build a workout plan, 38, beginner, 10lb dumbbells, 15 min/day"*
- AI returns standard answers
  - 10 squats, 10 push-ups
  - ✅ Sensible but ❌ uninspired

### How AI Generates Responses
- Trained on **internet text**
- Output has some inherent randomness
- 📊 Response distribution
  - **High probability** → common-sense (bicep curls)
  - **Medium probability** → slightly unique (yoga block on head)
  - **Low probability** → highly creative (cat-triggered micro-workouts)

### When Common-Sense Is Fine
- ✅ Factual questions
  - *"Tallest building?"* → **Burj Khalifa**
  - Average internet answer = correct answer
- ❌ Brainstorming
  - Average answer = boring answer

## Pushing AI Into Creative Territory

### Add Specific Context
- Include **unusual constraints & details**
- Example additions
  - Have a **mini trampoline**
  - Have a **cat**
  - Trouble staying motivated
  - *No squats*
- Result: custom, creative ideas
  - 💡 **Trampoline breaks**
  - 💡 **Cat-triggered micro-workouts**

### The Context Problem
- ⚠️ *Too much* possible context to share
- Question: **what should you prioritize telling the AI?**

## The Iteration Technique

### Step 1: Provide Background
- Example: debt-payoff brainstorming
  - $1,100 credit card debt @ **19%**
  - $40 monthly minimum
  - Student loan @ **8%**
  - $900 family loan

### Step 2: Ask for Multiple Options
- 📌 Request **3–5 options**, not one answer
- AI returns plans
  - Plan 1: *Liquidity first*
  - Plan 2: *Eliminate highest-interest loan*
  - Plan 3: *Pay family back first*

### Step 3: Give Targeted Feedback
- 💡 Feedback = the *most efficient* way to surface relevant context
- Tell AI what you like / dislike
  - ❌ "Option 1 too passive"
  - ✅ "Like paying off 19% loan"
- Add new context
  - "$450 cash incoming"
  - "Moving house soon"

### Step 4: Loop Until Satisfied
- Request **new options** informed by feedback
- Repeat: *options → feedback → options*
- Then ask AI to **flesh out** the winner

## The Brainstorming Recipe
- Give **relevant context** upfront
- Ask for a **handful of options**
- Give **feedback** on each option
- Ask for **more options**
- 🔁 Iterate several rounds
- Have AI elaborate on favorites

## Key Takeaways
- 💡 AI defaults to **common-sense answers** — great for facts, weak for creativity
- 📌 More **context** = more **creative & relevant** output
- 🔑 **Iteration with feedback** is the most efficient way to identify useful context
- ✅ Ask for **3–5 options**, never just one
- ✅ Treat AI as a **back-and-forth thought partner**, not a one-shot answer machine
- ⚠️ A single prompt rarely surfaces the best ideas — *loop and refine*
