# Writing with AI

## Why Writing Matters for AI
- 📊 **24%** of ChatGPT tasks are writing
  - Largest single task category (OpenAI study)
- 💡 Writing *is* thinking
  - Forces clarity of thought
  - AI's reasoning ability can amplify yours
- ⚠️ Naive "just write it for me" → **AI slop**

## What Is AI Slop?
- 🔑 **Definition**: AI-generated content that looks good on a glance but lacks substance
- Sentences sound fine *in isolation*
- Collectively feels shallow, thoughtless
- 📊 **40%** of US employees received "workslop" in the past month
- Vague, overly important-sounding lines
  - *"But it does change everything."*

### Telltale Signs of AI Writing
- **Em dash overuse** —
  - 📊 Trending up on Bluesky since ChatGPT launch
- **Overused words**
  - *delve*
  - *nuanced*
  - *robustly*
- **Lists of three** (overrepresented)
- **Fewer unique words** overall
- **Fewer nouns** → puffy adjective phrases
  - *"a robustly structured and highly insightful paper"*
- **"Not X but Y"** construction
  - *"It's not just about speed, it's about availability"*
  - *"It's not about infrastructure, it's about architecture"*
  - ⚠️ Often X and Y are both vague

### Reverse Contamination
- 💡 Humans now sound more like AI
- *delve* usage rising in **podcasts & talks**
  - In spontaneous speech, not just scripts
- People absorb patterns from heavy AI use

## Progressive Outlining: The Better Way
- 🔑 **Core idea**: Iterate on structure *before* prose
- ❌ Don't ask AI for final text on turn one
- ✅ Outline → refine → bullets → refine → text

### Step-by-Step Workflow
- **1. State the goal & POV**
  - Be explicit when prompt is non-neutral
  - Example: *"article arguing small AI teams move faster than large non-AI teams"*
- **2. Gather evidence**
  - Ask AI to research **for and against**
  - Let it search and surface sources
- **3. Brainstorm multiple outlines**
  - Request **3 outline options**
  - Add constraints (e.g., include counter-argument)
  - Provide your own context (uploaded stories, examples)
- **4. Give feedback on outlines**
  - Pick one, mix elements
  - Reorder sections (e.g., move thesis earlier)
  - Add analogies (e.g., *Pixar's Toy Story* — small team, first CG feature)
- **5. Expand headings to bullet points**
  - Still not prose
  - Iterate on bullets
- **6. Generate final text** *only at the end*

### Why Outlining First = High Leverage
- 💡 **Small outline edits → large text changes**
- Editing finished prose
  - Change a word → only that word changes
  - Rest of paragraph stays mediocre
- Editing the outline
  - Change a few words → whole section restructures
- 📌 Efficient way to **think through** what you actually want to say

## Working from Existing Text
- 📊 **~2/3** of writing chats start from pre-existing text (OpenAI data)
- Not from a blank page
- Use AI to **critique** and improve drafts
  - Covered in the next lesson (`07_ai_critique`)

## Key Takeaways
- 💡 **Writing is thinking** — use AI to sharpen the thinking, not skip it
- ⚠️ Asking AI to write the final draft first produces **slop**: em dashes, *delve*, "not X but Y", vague grandiosity
- ✅ **Progressive outlining** — outline → bullets → prose — is the antidote
- 🔑 Iterate where leverage is highest: **the outline**, not the finished sentences
- 📌 Be explicit about **POV and constraints** in your prompt; neutral prompts give neutral mush
- 💡 Heavy AI use is changing **human** speech too — guard your own voice
