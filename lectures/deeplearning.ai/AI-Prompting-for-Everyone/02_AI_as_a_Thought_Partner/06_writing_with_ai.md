# Writing with AI

## Why Writing + AI

- 📊 **24%** of ChatGPT tasks are writing
  - Single largest task category (OpenAI study)
- 💡 Writing *is* thinking
  - AI excels at thinking & reasoning
  - Can amplify your own thought process
- 📊 ~**2/3** of writing chats start from *existing text*
  - Not from a blank page
  - Critique & revision is a major use case

## The AI Slop Problem

### 🔑 What is AI Slop

- Content that **looks good if you don't read carefully**
- Each sentence sounds fine in isolation
- Collectively **lacks substance** & deep thought
- 📊 **40%** of US employees received "workslop" last month

### Telltale Signs

- **Em dash** overuse
  - Trending up on Bluesky since ChatGPT launch
- Overused words
  - *nuanced*
  - *delve*
- Overused patterns
  - Lists of three
  - Fewer nouns → puffed-up adjectives
    - *"robustly structured and highly insightful paper"*
  - **"Not X, but Y"** construction
    - *"not just about speed, it's about availability"*
    - *"not about infrastructure, it's about architecture"*
    - ⚠️ Often vague on both sides
  - Vague-but-grand sentences
    - *"But it does change everything."*

### Spillover Into Humans

- 💡 Humans now sound more like AI
- Use of *delve* rising in podcasts & talks
- True for **spontaneous** *and* prepared speech
  - Not just AI-written scripts
  - Speech patterns absorbed from heavy AI use

## Technique: Progressive Outlining

### 🔑 Core Idea

- ❌ Don't ask AI for final text immediately
- ✅ Iterate through **outline → bullets → prose**
- 📌 Refine the *structure* before the *words*

### Workflow Steps

- **1. State the goal & POV**
  - Example: *"small AI teams move faster than large non-AI teams"*
  - ⚠️ Acknowledge non-neutral prompts
- **2. Gather evidence**
  - Ask AI to research **for and against**
  - Web search for supporting articles
- **3. Brainstorm outline options**
  - Request **3 different outlines**
  - Ask for a **counter-argument** section
  - Upload your own stories / context
- **4. Give feedback on outlines**
  - *"Use option 1, keep all stories"*
  - *"Move thesis after story 1"*
  - Add analogies (e.g. **Pixar's Toy Story**, 1990s small team, first full CGI feature)
- **5. Expand to bullet points**
  - Not full prose yet
  - Iterate on bullets
- **6. Generate final text**
  - Only after outline & bullets feel right

### 💡 Why Outlining Wins

- **High leverage edits**
  - Change a few outline words → whole section rewrites
- Editing final prose only changes **one word at a time**
- Faster, more efficient review cycle
- Helps *you* clarify what you actually want to say

## Example: Flying Squirrel & Coconut

- Straight-to-prose approach
  - Edit one word → only that word changes
  - Paragraph structure stays stuck
- Outline-first approach
  - Tweak outline → entire paragraph reshapes
  - 📌 Demonstrates leverage of structural edits

## Key Takeaways

- 💡 **Writing is thinking** — use AI to think, not just to type
- ⚠️ Asking AI to write final text directly produces **AI slop**
- 🔑 **Progressive outlining**: outline → bullets → prose
- ✅ Edit structure first — it's the highest-leverage change
- 📌 Watch for slop tells: em dashes, *delve*, *nuanced*, "not X but Y", lists of three
- 💡 Humans are absorbing AI speech patterns — stay deliberate about your voice
- ✅ Most real writing work is **revising existing text**, not starting blank
