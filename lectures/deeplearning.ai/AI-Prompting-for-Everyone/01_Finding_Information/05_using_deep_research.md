# Using Deep Research

## What is Deep Research
- Synthesizes **many dozens of sources**
- Does extensive thinking for a deeply researched answer
- Available in **ChatGPT**, **Gemini**, **Claude**
- 💡 Valuable and often **underutilized** tool
- An example of **agentic AI**
  - 🔑 AI has flexibility to make decisions on what to do next
  - Decides what additional searches to carry out

## How It Works
### Step 1: Formulate Research Plan
- AI thinks through types of sources needed
- 📌 User can approve or edit the plan
- Often fine to launch without edits unless something looks wrong

### Step 2: Iterative Web Search Loop
- Issues **many web searches in parallel**
  - ✅ Efficient — fetches many pages at once
  - Not one-at-a-time
- Reads and synthesizes web pages
- Assesses source **relevance**
- Decides whether to search again
  - May use different search terms
  - Loosely follows original plan
  - Flexibility to dig deeper into areas

### Step 3: Synthesize Final Report
- Summarizes all downloaded pages
- Adds **citations**
- Presents detailed report with sections

## Example: Halloween Haunted House
- Prompt includes rich context
  - Location (Palo Alto)
  - Front yard size
  - Desired experience
- AI searches in sequence
  - Local permits & Halloween ordinances
  - Fire safety guidelines
  - Decoration ideas
- Output: structured plan
  - Structural & regulatory framework
  - Safety considerations
- **Gemini** bonus feature
  - 💡 Convert research into **web page** or **infographic**
  - Pie charts, visualizations, checklists

## Web Search vs Deep Research

### Web Search
- Single question, quick answer
- Work that takes **seconds**
- Handful of sources
- ✅ Triggered **automatically or by user**
- Good for
  - "Find me a highly rated gym"
  - "Weather in Dubai this week"

### Deep Research
- Complex, multi-dimensional questions
- Work that takes **minutes to hours** manually
- Synthesizes **many sources**
- ⚠️ Triggered **explicitly by user** in the UI
- Good for
  - Impact of daily steps on long-term health
  - How weather affects tourism in Dubai
  - Anything requiring **multiple views** synthesized

## Three Paths for Finding Information

### Pretrained Knowledge
- No online lookup
- Answer in **seconds**
- Best for
  - Basic facts
  - Definitions
  - Common summaries
- Example: *"I dropped my phone in soup"*

### Web Search
- Downloads a **handful of sources**
- Relatively **up-to-date** info
- Takes **many seconds**

### Deep Research
- Downloads **dozens or more sources**
- Up-to-date information
- Takes **many minutes or longer**
- 💡 Great for synthesizing many knowledge sources

## Key Takeaways
- 🔑 **Deep research = agentic AI** that plans, searches, evaluates, and synthesizes autonomously
- 📌 Use **pretrained knowledge** for instant facts, **web search** for fresh single answers, **deep research** for complex synthesis
- ⚠️ Deep research must be **triggered explicitly** — it costs minutes of waiting
- 💡 Parallel web searches make deep research **efficient** despite breadth
- ✅ Providing rich **context** in the prompt yields a far better research plan
- 📌 Build intuition on **when** to use each path — it's the core skill of this module
