# AI Novice vs. AI Power User

## Context: Why Prompting Matters in 2026
- Prompting AI is **very different** from 2022 ChatGPT era
- 💡 One of the **most impactful skills** to develop today
- Today's tools far more powerful than even a year ago
- Goal: move from novice → power user

## Asking Hard Questions (Not Just Google Searches)
### Novice Pattern
- Treats AI like a **search engine**
- Short factual queries
  - *e.g., "Does Taco Bell still have the double decker taco?"*
- Fine for trivia, but underuses the model
### Power User Pattern
- Tackles **complex, multi-document problems**
- Example: **car-buying decision**
  - Upload car specs, quotes, insurance plans
  - Ask for **trade-off analysis**
  - 📌 Tell AI to *"read everything and think hard before answering"*
- Lets AI think for **seconds to minutes**
- Output: detailed report → 💡 huge time saver
### Tools That Support This
- ChatGPT
- Gemini
- Anthropic's Claude
- Other commercial services

## Providing Rich Context
### The Mental Model
- 💡 Treat AI like a **smart fresh college grad**
  - Highly motivated
  - Doesn't know you yet
- Have *empathy* for the AI without over-anthropomorphizing
- 🔑 Ask: *"Do they have enough info to do a good job?"*
### Novice Pattern
- ❌ Short prompt, hopes AI fills in blanks
- Example: *"Write a self-review to send to my boss"*
- Result: generic, unhelpful output
### Power User Pattern
- ✅ Upload supporting context, e.g.
  - Screenshot of project tracker
  - Recent project docs
  - Voice memo notes about projects
- AI can capture what you're **most proud of**

## Getting Honest Feedback (Avoiding Sycophancy)
### The Problem
- ⚠️ AI often **wants to please you**
- Many systems trained to make users happy
- 🔑 **Sycophancy** — reflecting back your preferences
- Even a *hint* of desired answer biases the response
### Novice Pattern
- ❌ Loaded prompt
  - *"I have a **great** business idea, mobile tie-dyeing. Critique it."*
- AI naturally agrees it's great
### Power User Pattern
- ✅ **Neutral phrasing** — no hints of hoped-for answer
- ✅ Provide a **rubric / grading criteria**
  - Is there a problem?
  - Is there a market?
  - Do I have a competitive advantage?
- Example prompt
  - *"Analyze this business idea **objectively**: mobile tie-dyeing. Use the rubric above."*
- Result: honest score (e.g., *"8 out of 100"*) + reasoning + useful follow-up questions

## Writing with AI (Iterative Workflow)
### Novice Pattern
- ❌ *"Write a blog post about the BlackBerry"*
- Output: **AI slop**
  - Generic
  - Uninteresting
  - Bloated text
### Power User Pattern — Stepwise Iteration
- 1. **Outline first**
  - *"Outline a blog post about the BlackBerry based on my notes"*
- 2. **Critique & iterate** the outline
  - Several back-and-forth rounds
- 3. Expand outline → **bullet points**
  - Critique and refine again
- 4. Only then → **draft the final article**
- 💡 Treat AI as a **thinking partner** for brainstorming
- ✅ Produces text you're actually happy with

## Reality Check on AI Mistakes
- AI **does** make mistakes — but fewer than most people think
- Big improvement since 2022–2023
- ⚠️ Viral examples skew perception
  - *"How many R's in strawberry?"* → answers 2
  - *"Walk or drive to wash my car?"* → says walk
- 📌 Viral failures are **not representative** of real capabilities
- High-value tasks AI handles well today
  - Deep research & research reports
  - Personal data analysis (health, heart rate, running times)
  - Building websites *(covered later)*

## Why Becoming a Power User Pays Off
- Saves **time** in work and life
- Improves professional **and** personal outcomes
- Enables building **cool things**
- 📌 **In-demand job skill** across every role
- Bonus: using AI well is genuinely *fun*

## Key Takeaways
- 💡 **Hard questions + rich context** unlock AI's real power, not Google-style queries
- 🔑 Treat AI like a **smart new hire** — give it the background it needs
- ⚠️ Avoid **sycophancy** with neutral prompts and explicit rubrics
- ✅ For writing: **outline → critique → bullets → draft**, not one-shot generation
- 📌 Viral AI failures overstate today's error rate — modern models excel at research, analysis, and building
- 💡 Power-user prompting is a **transferable, high-leverage skill** for any career
